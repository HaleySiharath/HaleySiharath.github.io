What is CRAP?

The principles of design, CRAP, is an extrodinary tool used by graphic designers to create designers that reels in consumer/readers. CRAP stands for contrast, repetition, alignment, proximity.

Contrast refers to both text size and color of the design, it allows the reader to easily read and navigate the page. Repetition is used in design to keep a theme ongoing. It also unifies a design making it ideal for branding. Alignment is the lines that run through the work. This can be text alignment or the placement of pictures. Proper alignment will help avoid the creation of a confusing mumble jumble design. The last design principle, proximity, breaks up the content of a design to allow for the consumer/reader to easily skim the information off of the deisgn.
