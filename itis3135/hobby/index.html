Types of Roller Skating

Everyone does not skate the same. It is fair to say that every skater has their own style of skating. Some common styles is 'style' skating and speed skating. 'Style' skating encompasses a dance like feeling. There is moving and grooving to the beat with tons of spins and backskating involved. As with dancing, different regions have different styles and it translates into the moves you can see in the rink. On the other hand, speed skating is when a skater skates as fast as they can.

Not to limit skating to these two categories, as there are many more, but they are the most common skating styles seen in the rink. In order to give a better idea of skating, the two most common types of skates are described below.

Quads

Quads are the typical skate that most think of when they hear roller skating. They are easier to balance in and there are slight variations of the skate that can make it yours. For instance, wheels can be chunky or skinny and the shoes can be high or low tops.

Common activitis done in quads are the following:

  • Roller durby
  • Style skating
  • Parkour skating

Inlines

Inline skates refer to skates that have all of the wheels in one line. Common types of inline skates are roller blades. Inlines have varrying size of wheels and with the increased size of wheel the faster you are at skating.

How to Roller Skate

As with everything, skating takes practice, time, and dedication. There are hundreds of tutorials on the web showing you how to skate, but my best advice is to go out there and try your best. Finding your balance is key and with progress you can begin to focus on different styles of skating.

Where to Buy Your Skates

Searching for a skate that best fits you is a daunting task as skates are not cheap. In order to get the best quality expierience out of your skates, I reccomend going through these series of questions/steps.

  1. Do I want inline or quads?
  2. What is my price range?
  3. Do i prefer hightops or lowtops?
  4. Are my skates intended for indoor or outdoor skating?
  5. Does my local skate shop have roller skates?

These questions should help you narrow down what and where do you want to buy from. My only advice is to make an investment into these skates. They typically last from 5 to 8 years.
