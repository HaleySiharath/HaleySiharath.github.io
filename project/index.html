Ford Mustang Overview

The Ford Mustang was first on the market on April 17, 1964 at the price of $2,368. The car was instanly a hit with over 20,000 Mustangs being ordered on the first day alone. After being featured on the track for the 1964 Indianapolis 500, the popularity of Mustangs only grew. Over the years, the sporty coupe became the "it" car as it was increasingly being featured on films, commerical advertisements, and posters.

A large apeal to the Mustang is not only it's sleek style or it's performance abilities but the fact that it is an American made car. Overtime, mustangs have gone over an array of changes from generation to generation. Wether it be changes to the engine, such as in the sixth generation, or a redesign of the body, like the thrid generation.

Ford Mustang's Founders

The original team for the Ford Mustang consisted of executive stylist John Najjar, Ford stylist Philip T. Clark, and Ford Division market research manager Robert J. Eggert. Although these people had many teams working on the creation of Mustang, their work is to be appriceated for what the Mustang has become today.

Ford Mustang's by Generation

  • Generation 1

    The first generation began in 1965 and ended in 1973. The orignial Mustang was derived from the 1960 Ford Falcon compact. It was made public at the New York World's Fair. The intial model included a two body style car that came in either a hardtop or convertible. Newer models in 1969 changes the body design and introduced new trim and performance packages.

  • Generation 2

    The second generation began in 1974 and ended in 1978. This generation introudced the "Pintostang" which was smaller and more fuel-efficent. The 1978 model year changed from the fuel-efficient Mustang gerneation 2 with the flashy Cobra II and King Cobra Mustang.

  • Generation 3

    The third generation began in 1979 and ended in 1993. It introduced the Fox Body Mustang which was a redesign of the previous generation. The Fox Body Mustang kept the traditional long hood and shor rear deck but added European-style design. This included rectangular headlights and more cargo space.

  • Generation 4

    The fourth generation began in 1994 and ended in 2004. This generation marked the 30th anniversary of the Original Mustang. To commemeroate the anniversary, Ford introduced a new lineup of models that resembled the first gerneration. In 2015, the Mustang faced a complete redesign that was based on the Fox platform.

  • Generation 5

    The fifth generation began in 2005 and ended in 2014. This generation resembled the styling of the first-generation Mustang. The five-speed manual transmission remained the standard for the series with the option of a fice-speed automatic transmission. The 2010 model featured a redeisgned exteriror with sequential LED taillights.

  • Generation 6

    The sixth generation began in 2015 and is ongoing till today. These models fearure an 2.3-liter EcoBoost four-cylinder enginge and a rear suspension system. Changes include a wider body, inceased passenger space, a new grille, and new exterior colors.
