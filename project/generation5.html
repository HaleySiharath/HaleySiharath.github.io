Generation 5

The fifth generation began in 2005 and ended in 2014. This generation resembled the styling of the first-generation Mustang. The five-speed manual transmission remained the standard for the series with the option of a fice-speed automatic transmission. The 2010 model featured a redeisgned exteriror with sequential LED taillights.

The Shelby GT500, designed by Carroll Shelby, had a 5.4-liter suercharged V8 block enginer and an output of 500-horsepower. In 2011, the engines and transmission options were revised with the introduction of a six-speed manual and automatic transmission. In 2012, the Mustang Boss 302 emerged and in 2013 an update was made to teh Shelby Mustang GT500. The update included a larger 5.8-liter supercharged V8 engine producing 662-horsepower.

Shelby Mustang GT500 became the first Mustang to produce over 600-horsepower

2010 redesigned exterior reduced drag coefficient on base and GT models

2011 replaced hydraulic steering with electric power steering
